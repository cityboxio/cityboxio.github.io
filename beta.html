philosophy

 Urban-innovation aims to fulfill amenities' - ever escalating - demand. However, fully utilizing apropos urban-tech data/services for a specific geolocation and its associative urban cultures is a strenuous endeavor. withdrawing away from the compliexities and focusing on conceptual level of what a city is, what a worldcity is. Cultivating an openculture is our key to innovation. the ouroboros we are introducing the term "urban sandboxing" as our most quintessential concept for tackling this problem. urban sandboxing is our solution. In its totality World cities shape our humanity. we focus on understanding them to know how to steer our cultures.

whenever there is opendata, innovation is possible, and even probable. innovation requires experimenting. experimenting requires colloberation within an openculture at a certain level of focus. collberation requires aiding tools. citybox is an aiding tool for collberation among experiming startups for urban-innovation on a city level-of-focus. worldcity status is important. citybox aim to connect startups focusing on a speficic city to turn it into a worldcity.
We scope our attribution of the undertaking difficulties to three primarily keyed problems: * The adhoc methodologies of urban-innovation data/service acquisition regarding a specific geolocation - perceived as a (Geo)Search Problem. * The vast interdisciplinary scope of urban-innovation as a domain of knowledge - perceived as a Data Consolidation Problem. * The time-value characteristics of urban-innovation data/services - perceived as a CAP Therom/Real-time Data Analytics Problem. In this paper, we propose the design of a geo-oriented distributed ledger technology aka "blockchain" for urban-innovation, via researching various blockchain designs in other domains. And we set our criteria for evaluating our proposed design, on its technical viability as a solution to our perceived problems.
